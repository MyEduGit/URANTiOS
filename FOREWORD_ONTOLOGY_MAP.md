# FOREWORD_ONTOLOGY_MAP

## Ontology Type
A **multi-axial, stratified, typed metaphysical ontology** with both existential and processual dimensions.

## Core Classes
- Deity
- Divinity (quality field)
- God (personalized Deity references)
- First/Second/Third Source and Center
- Paradise
- Deity/Universal/Unqualified Absolutes
- Personal beings (prepersonal, personal, superpersonal)
- Reality levels (finite, absonite, absolute)

## Key Distinctions

### Existential vs Experiential
- **Existential**: beginningless, ontically given.
- **Experiential**: realized through process/participation.

### Personal vs Impersonal
- Personal: intentional, relational, addressable.
- Impersonal: pattern-power-law fields/grounds.

### Actual vs Potential
- Potential exists as unrealized but ontologically relevant.
- Actual emerges via creation/eventuation/experiential realization.

### Finite vs Absonite vs Absolute
- Finite: time-space bounded, evolutionary.
- Absonite: time-space transcending without absoluteness.
- Absolute: timeless/spaceless totality mode.

### Deity vs Non-Deity Domains
- Deity: personalizable unity source.
- Non-Deity: impersonal reality constituents and absolute conditions.

## How this differs from common ontologies
- **Versus philosophy ontologies**: more jurisdictional and operationally prescriptive.
- **Versus theology systems**: heavier formal categorization and explicit level architecture.
- **Versus CS ontologies**: includes normatively loaded value fields (truth/beauty/goodness) and supra-empirical classes.
